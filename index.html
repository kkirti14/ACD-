02 · The Framework

ACD explained.
Once. Completely.

Five original constructs. One governance law. One proportionality principle. The three-layer governance model. Each is introduced once in this document — subsequent chapters apply them without re-explaining.

The three-layer governance model
A system can be fully compliant with Layers 1 and 2 and still commit catastrophic cognitive governance violations. ACD is Layer 3 — the only layer that governs the gap between machine processing and human conscious authorship.
Layer 1 · Technical
Can the device?
MCP, A2A, Bluetooth, HealthKit, OAuth, 6G. Governs interoperability. Has no concept of human conscious bandwidth.

Cannot prevent cognitively harmful actions.
Layer 2 · Privacy Law
May the device?
GDPR, HIPAA, India DPDP Act 2023, CCPA. Governs data rights. Does not govern what AI does with compliant data across the gap.

Cannot prevent authorship violations.
Layer 3 · ACD
Should the AI act?
AAA Protocol, CTS, Non-Delegable Registry, Kamble's Law. Governs the gap itself — machine channel to human channel.

Does not yet exist as a formal standard. ACD fills it.

The worked failure case (Layer 3 only catches this): A phone AI combines biometric, calendar, and messaging data — all Layer 1 and 2 compliant. It concludes the user is overwhelmed. It drafts and sends a message to their manager requesting extended leave. Zero bits of the 120-bit human channel were engaged. Layer 1: compliant. Layer 2: compliant. Layer 3 verdict: catastrophic cognitive governance violation.

The five ACD constructs
Construct 01
Agency–Autonomy–Authorship Matrix
The first framework that makes AI autonomy a design variable, not an engineering decision. Every AI feature is placed in one cell before design begins. Agency: who decides. Autonomy: who acts. Authorship: who owns the outcome.

User-led: user initiates, user authors. Co-led: AI proposes, user confirms. AI-led: within pre-authorised, legible scope.
Construct 02
Cognitive Transparency Score (CTS)
The first composite measurement instrument for cognitive quality. CTS = f(CL · TF · AI · RE · SC).

CL: Cognitive Load (NASA-TLX, EEG, pupillometry). TF: Trust Formation. AI: Attentional Integration. RE: Retention. SC: System Compliance.

Gold ≥85 · Silver 70–84 · Bronze 55–69.
Construct 03
Cognitive Inventory
The pre-design assessment of the user's actual cognitive state before any design decision. Six dimensions: attentional budget, prior load, trust history, ambient ecology, cognitive delta, cognitive personality profile.

Design begins here — not with the brief.
Construct 04
Cognitive Mediation Gradient
Six stages from Passive Recording to Cognitive Substitution. Kamble's Law activates at Stage 4 (Proactive Mediation). JioFrames currently operates at Stage 3. All major ambient AI platforms are drifting toward Stage 4–5 without governance architecture.
Construct 05 · Kamble's Cognitive Governance Law
Four non-negotiable conditions. Falsifiable. Empirically testable.
1. Human intent sovereignty: intent formed in the 120-bit channel remains sovereign over all machine-channel processing.
2. Non-delegable domain protection: no machine-channel processing substitutes for human conscious authorship in protected domains.
3. Structural contestability: the human can at any moment understand what was processed, halt further processing, and reverse what has been done.
4. Governance context propagation: the full AAA Protocol state propagates intact through every agent, device, and delegation. No handoff leaves sovereignty behind.
The Proportionality Principle
The most important governance principle emerging from the cognitive gap: as AI processing power increases, the transparency of its mediation logic must increase proportionally. This is not a trade-off. It is a non-negotiable scaling relationship.
Transparency Obligation ∝ Processing Scale × Implicitness Rate
The more powerful the AI and the more implicitly it acts, the more precisely and accessibly it must disclose what it has done. As capability doubles every 18 months, so must the granularity of disclosure.

05 · Design System · ACD.DS + ACD.CDR

The cognitive design
language.

Material Design governs pixels. Apple HIG governs platform conventions. ACD.DS governs cognition — the design surface, the currency (bits not pixels), and the output (trust not delight). This chapter is the reference for designers building ACD-governed products.

Timing tokens — governance, not animation
--t-signal · 300ms minimum
Predictability signal window
Every autonomous AI action must be preceded by a 300–800ms signal. Below 300ms: imperceptible. Above 800ms: creates anticipatory anxiety. This window is governance, not UX polish.
--t-dissolve · 3000ms · --t-max-persist · 8000ms
Dissolution rules
Peripheral information dissolves after 3s without engagement. No AI element persists beyond 8s without interaction. Violation: attentional budget depleted. CTS Cognitive Load drops below target.
